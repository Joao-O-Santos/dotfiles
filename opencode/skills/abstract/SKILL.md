# Skill: abstract

#style-core

## Structure

### Empirical Papers (structured abstract)

1. **Background** – 1–2 sentences: what gap or question motivated the study
2. **Objective** – what this paper does (not what the field needs)
3. **Method** – design, participants/data, key measures in ≤2 sentences
4. **Results** – primary findings with effect sizes where possible; no interpretation
5. **Conclusion** – 1 sentence: what the findings mean and for whom

### Theoretical Papers

1. **Problem** – what conceptual issue the paper addresses
2. **Approach** – the argumentative strategy or framework used
3. **Contribution** – the core claim and what it adds to the literature
4. **Implications** – what researchers or practitioners should do differently

## Rules

- Write the abstract last, after the paper is complete
- Every sentence must be self-contained; no forward references ("see below")
- Do not introduce concepts not in the paper
- Results: numbers > vague qualifiers ("significantly" without a statistic is weak)
- 150–250 words for most journals; confirm target word count before drafting
- No citations in abstracts unless journal explicitly permits them

## Workflow

1. Extract one sentence per section from the paper
2. Draft abstract from those extractions
3. Tighten to word limit without losing specifics
4. Check: can a reader fully understand the study from this alone?
