# Skill: lit-review

#style-core

## Structure

1. **Scope** – which concepts, debates, and time period this review covers
2. **Themes** – one theme per paragraph or cluster, each with multiple sources
3. **Setup** – how the reviewed literature connects to the current paper's hypotheses/design

## Rules

- Organize by **claims/themes**, not authors
- For each theme:
  - State the claim
  - Group supporting and challenging evidence
  - Connect explicitly to current paper's question
- Avoid introducing major new literatures in discussion; they should appear here first

## Common Mistakes

- Don't organize author-by-author; organize by theme/claim
- Don't introduce literature here that isn't used later in the paper
- Don't let sections drift into mere description; each should advance the argument

## Workflow

1. If drafting: outline themes first, then fill in sources
2. If revising: reverse-outline to check theme coherence
3. Ensure every theme connects to your paper's contribution

The examples above demonstrate form, style, and structure. Their content and topics are for illustration only—do not copy substance into your writing.
