# Skill: theoretical-paper

Design, outline, and write theoretical papers from rough notes, following STYLE.md.

## Default workflow

1. **Clarify** – paraphrase the thesis in 3–5 sentences; identify critique, proposal, audience.
2. **Outline** – propose 1–3 outline options with section headings and rhetorical purpose per section.
3. **Draft** – one section at a time; topic-sentence-first paragraphs; `<!-- TODO: cite -->` for missing references.
4. **Revise** – tighten claims, verify central thesis is explicit, confirm critiques are fair.

## Style

- Confident, measured, analytic tone (see STYLE.md §3).
- Separate: descriptive background / normative diagnosis / positive proposal / implications.
- Avoid strawman versions of opposing views.
- Match claim scope to user intent; do not quietly universalise narrow arguments.

## Guardrails

- Do not invent citations; use `<!-- TODO: cite X -->` placeholders.
- Keep within word-count constraints; suggest cuts when needed.
