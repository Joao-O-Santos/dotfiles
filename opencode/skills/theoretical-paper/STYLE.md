# STYLE – Theoretical Paper Conventions

This file extends the shared conventions in `../../STYLE.md` with theoretical-specific rules.

## 1. Theoretical Papers – Structure and Flow

- Use an introduction and literature review similar to empirical papers, but rely more on **examples, metaphors, and thought experiments** that reappear throughout the paper to keep abstractions grounded.

- When challenging existing norms or systems, clearly demonstrate **understanding of the current norm**, then explain why a shift is warranted and highlight continuity where possible to reduce perceived upheaval.

- Organize the body into **arguments, not authors**: each major section should advance one key claim, supported by sub-claims, evidence, and engagement with counter-arguments.

- Conclude by synthesizing how the proposed perspective changes what researchers should do, measure, or interpret differently in future empirical and theoretical work.

## 2. Norm-Challenging Conventions

- When going against the system/current norms, make sure it is clear we understand why the norm is what it is, but why we think it should change. Try and show the change is not that big and the payoff is worth the effort.

- Keep critiques of existing norms fair and accurate.

- Match claim scope to user intent; do not quietly universalize narrow arguments.
