# Examples of Theoretical Paper Intros

## Example 1: Replication Crisis

*"Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood."* (Marie Curie)

The replication crisis created a climate of uncertainty, provoking emotional reactions and radical calls to action. Some critics argued for improving statistical models, others for restructuring incentive structures.

We believe the debate largely ignored insights from the philosophy of science, particularly the New Experimentalism. We draw from foundational theories to present recommendations for overcoming the crisis.

## Example 2: Normative Ethics

"Is it ever right to lie?" This question has occupied philosophers for millennia, with deontologists and consequentialists offering starkly different answers.

We argue that both traditions miss a crucial dimension: the social context in which moral judgments are made. By examining how norms emerge and evolve, we can better understand the foundations of ethical reasoning.

In this paper, we develop a framework for normative ethics that accounts for social embeddedness, showing how this perspective resolves longstanding debates.
